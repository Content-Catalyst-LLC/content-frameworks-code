# Modeling Principles

1. AIDA is a persuasive-sequence model, not a complete theory of decision-making.
2. Attention should create relevant salience, not clickbait.
3. Interest should develop context, relevance, and credibility.
4. Desire should be supported by evidence, proportionate value, and fit language.
5. Action should be clear, feasible, transparent, and respectful of agency.
6. Stage balance matters because overemphasis on attention or action can weaken trust.
7. Ethical-risk checks should flag false urgency, exaggeration, hidden conditions, and agency reduction.
8. Conversion metrics should not override clarity, accessibility, or trust.
9. Readiness scores are review signals, not proof of persuasion quality.
10. Human editorial and ethical review remain necessary.
