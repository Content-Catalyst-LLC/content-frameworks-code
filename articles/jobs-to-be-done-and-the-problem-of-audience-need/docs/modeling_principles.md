# Modeling Principles

1. Audience need is not the same as demographic identity.
2. A job is the progress someone is trying to make in a situation.
3. Content should be reviewed against the job it claims to support.
4. Functional, emotional, social, strategic, and learning jobs may all matter.
5. Job statements should include situation, motivation, desired outcome, and constraint.
6. Content fit depends on format, examples, sections, and next-step alignment.
7. JTBD assumptions should be supported by evidence, not internal imagination alone.
8. Audience agency requires alternatives, bounded claims, and transparent next steps.
9. Metrics should be tied to job completion, not only traffic or clicks.
10. Governance should track job assumptions, evidence status, review dates, and revisions.
