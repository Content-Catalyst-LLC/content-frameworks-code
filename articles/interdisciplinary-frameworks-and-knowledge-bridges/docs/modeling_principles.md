# Modeling Principles

1. Interdisciplinary work must preserve domain difference instead of flattening it.
2. Shared terms should be defined by source and target domain.
3. Evidence compatibility depends on method fit and claim type.
4. Concepts carry assumptions when they move across fields.
5. Boundary objects coordinate meaning but are not neutral.
6. Audience support is necessary for public interdisciplinary explanation.
7. Misuse warnings should identify where a concept transfer breaks down.
8. Governance is needed because disciplinary standards and vocabulary drift.
9. Bridge-readiness scores are review signals, not proof of intellectual validity.
10. Human domain, ethical, and editorial review remain necessary.
