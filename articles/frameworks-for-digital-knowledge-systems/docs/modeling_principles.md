# Modeling Principles

1. A digital knowledge system is more than a website or archive.
2. Content becomes knowledge architecture through structure, metadata, links, and governance.
3. Taxonomy answers where a record belongs.
4. Metadata answers what the system must know about a record.
5. Internal links express relationships among concepts and pages.
6. Repositories support reproducibility when technical examples, code, data, or schemas are used.
7. Search depends on structured records, labels, metadata, and content quality.
8. Accessibility should be built into templates and governance.
9. AI-assisted systems require review gates, evidence checks, and audit trails.
10. System health should be inspected across coverage, metadata, links, repositories, review currency, and governance.
