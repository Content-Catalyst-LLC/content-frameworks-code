# Modeling Principles

1. Evidence architecture connects claims to support, not just articles to reference lists.
2. Claim type should shape the evidence standard.
3. Claim strength should match source authority and directness of support.
4. Citations should be connected to specific claims.
5. Uncertainty and limitation language should be visible where interpretation depends on it.
6. Visuals make claims and should be audited as evidence structures.
7. Source quantity is not the same as source quality or source fit.
8. Review readiness matters because evidence ages and claims drift.
9. Evidence-readiness scores are governance signals, not proof of truth.
10. Human editorial and domain review remain necessary.
