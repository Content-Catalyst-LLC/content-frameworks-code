# Content Framework Modeling Principles

## 1. Define framework purpose
A framework should clarify what it is designed to organize.

## 2. Distinguish framework levels
A persuasive sequence, knowledge taxonomy, message architecture, and strategic-analysis model do different work.

## 3. Preserve editorial judgment
Scoring and metadata help maintain content systems, but they cannot replace expert interpretation.

## 4. Treat links as structure
Internal links are not decoration. They express relationships among concepts.

## 5. Track evidence and references
Strong content systems preserve the link between claims, sources, and interpretive context.

## 6. Avoid framework drift
Frameworks should be reviewed so they do not become empty labels.

## 7. Keep templates flexible
Reusable templates should guide structure without forcing formulaic writing.
