# Modeling Principles

1. A curriculum pathway is a learning route, not just an article list.
2. Article maps become stronger when they include prerequisite logic.
3. Learning objectives should describe what learners can explain, compare, apply, evaluate, or create.
4. Scaffolding should reduce avoidable confusion without removing meaningful complexity.
5. Feedback and assessment should reveal whether the pathway supports understanding.
6. Transfer support helps learners apply knowledge beyond the original article.
7. Accessibility is part of pathway design, not a later compliance layer.
8. Governance keeps pathways accurate as articles, references, and repositories change.
9. Pathway-readiness scores are review signals, not proof of learning quality.
10. Human educational and editorial judgment remains necessary.
