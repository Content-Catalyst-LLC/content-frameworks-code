# Modeling Principles

1. PAS and BAB are persuasive-sequence models, not complete theories of persuasion.
2. The problem or before state should be specific, accurate, and proportionate.
3. Agitation should clarify stakes, not escalate fear beyond evidence.
4. The after state should be credible, bounded, and supported.
5. The solution or bridge should explain the mechanism of movement.
6. Transformation claims should be supported by evidence, example, method, or reasoning.
7. Ethical-risk checks should flag invented pain, fear escalation, false urgency, and agency reduction.
8. Conversion metrics should not override trust, accessibility, or proportionality.
9. Readiness scores are review signals, not proof of persuasion quality.
10. Human editorial and ethical review remain necessary.
