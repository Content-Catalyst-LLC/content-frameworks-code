# Modeling Principles

1. A taxonomy is a conceptual structure, not just a tag list.
2. Every article should have one primary category.
3. Secondary categories and facets should support cross-cutting relationships.
4. Category boundaries should define what belongs and what does not.
5. Tags need governance to prevent sprawl.
6. Taxonomy and metadata should be designed together.
7. Category balance should be reviewed but not treated as a simple quality score.
8. Some categories are intentionally broad and some are intentionally specialized.
9. AI-assisted taxonomy suggestions require human validation.
10. Taxonomy governance should define creation, merging, renaming, deprecation, and retirement rules.
