# Modeling Principles

1. Persuasive frameworks are not ethically neutral when they shape attention, emotion, trust, urgency, and action.
2. Responsible persuasion preserves informed choice, refusal, comparison, and delay.
3. Pressure cues should be real, proportionate, and visible.
4. Evidence support should match the strength and type of each persuasive claim.
5. Social proof, authority cues, testimonials, and adoption numbers should be contextualized.
6. Dark patterns should be removed, not optimized.
7. Audience vulnerability requires additional transparency and reduced pressure.
8. Accessibility is part of ethical persuasion.
9. Interface design is part of persuasive ethics.
10. Governance should document reviewers, evidence, risk flags, revisions, and publication decisions.
