# Modeling Principles

1. Define framework purpose before choosing format.
2. Separate topic, outline, template, model, method, and framework.
3. Treat article maps as editorial models of knowledge domains.
4. Track metadata so pages can be audited, maintained, and reused.
5. Use taxonomies to clarify categories and conceptual boundaries.
6. Use internal links as semantic relationships, not decoration.
7. Attach evidence and limitations to major framework claims.
8. Treat framework design as editorial judgment, not a template shortcut.
9. Monitor framework drift, duplication, stale examples, and missing coverage.
10. Keep AI-assisted framework work subordinate to human editorial responsibility.
