# Modeling Principles

1. Framework value should be evaluated by use: comprehension, comparison, retention, action, governance, evidence integrity, audience fit, and ethical safety.
2. Research frameworks should distinguish evidence, interpretation, uncertainty, implication, and recommendation.
3. Educational frameworks should sequence concepts according to prerequisite knowledge and cognitive load.
4. Strategic communication frameworks should align audience need, central claims, proof points, and responsible action.
5. Frameworks should reduce unnecessary confusion without hiding necessary complexity.
6. Frameworks should be evaluated for ethical risk, including overclaiming, manipulation, false universality, and category distortion.
7. Metadata and internal-link structures should make framework systems auditable.
8. Computational workflows should support editorial judgment, not replace it.
9. Professional product workflows should preserve audit trails and produce machine-readable exports.
10. Catalyst Canvas-style workflows should be modular, reproducible, and review-ready.
