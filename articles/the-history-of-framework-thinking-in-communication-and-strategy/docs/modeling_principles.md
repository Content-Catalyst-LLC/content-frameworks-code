# Modeling Principles

1. Frameworks have histories.
2. Historical lineage affects responsible use.
3. A framework transferred across domains should carry use conditions and limitations.
4. Rhetorical frameworks emphasize persuasion and audience adaptation.
5. Educational frameworks emphasize sequence, cognitive load, learning progression, and transfer.
6. Scientific models emphasize representation, assumptions, scope, and limitations.
7. Management frameworks emphasize analysis, alignment, competition, goals, and measurement.
8. Information architecture emphasizes organization, labels, navigation, findability, and metadata.
9. AI-assisted frameworks require classification, evidence review, ethical review, and governance.
10. Historical awareness should support adaptation, not nostalgia.
