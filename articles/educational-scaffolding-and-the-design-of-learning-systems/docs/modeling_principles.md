# Modeling Principles

1. Learning systems require sequence, not just content lists.
2. Prerequisite knowledge should be visible and linked.
3. Scaffolding should reduce unnecessary cognitive burden without removing meaningful complexity.
4. Worked examples should make hidden expert judgment visible.
5. Feedback prompts help learners evaluate understanding.
6. Transfer support helps learners adapt frameworks beyond the original example.
7. Accessibility is part of scaffolding, not a separate afterthought.
8. Article maps can function as learning pathways when sequence and dependency are explicit.
9. Scaffold-readiness scores are governance signals, not proof of learning.
10. Learning systems should support guided independence rather than template dependence.
