# Modeling Principles

1. Research communication should distinguish claims, evidence, reasoning, methods, uncertainty, and implications.
2. Claim language should match evidence strength.
3. Method visibility is part of responsible interpretation.
4. Uncertainty should be explained, not hidden.
5. Audience translation should preserve evidence boundaries.
6. Visual explanation should support accessibility and avoid misleading precision.
7. Evidence type and source authority should be classified.
8. Public implications should be separated from evidence claims.
9. Research communication readiness scores are governance signals, not proof of truth.
10. Human editorial and domain review remain necessary.
