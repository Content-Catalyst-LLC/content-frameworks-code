# Modeling Principles

1. Internal links should be treated as semantic relationships, not decorative references.
2. Every important published article should have a path from the article map or a major hub.
3. Relationship type should be explicit.
4. Anchor text should describe the destination and relationship.
5. Link graph metrics should support editorial judgment, not replace it.
6. Orphaned published articles should trigger governance review.
7. Link-depth estimates should identify hidden or hard-to-discover content.
8. Cluster coherence should be balanced with bridge links.
9. Internal-link governance should be updated as articles are renamed, revised, published, or retired.
10. Product-grade workflows should produce audit trails and machine-readable exports.
